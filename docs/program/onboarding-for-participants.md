# Part 2: Software Applications

## Onboarding Process for Part 2: Software Applications

Congratulations on being accepted to Techtonica! Here are the things we need you to do before the program starts. Please check off each to-do when you've completed it or it doesn't apply to you.

## Pre-Onboarding (due Monday, December 29th)

### For participants receiving stipends only: complete forms for stipends

- If you don't have a bank account, we recommend opening a high-yield savings account, like CapitalOne's 360 Performance Savings.
- Download the blank W-9.
- Fill it out (you can use the camera in Preview for the signature).
- Save the document with your name at the beginning of the title, like so: "Jane Doe 2023 W-9."
- Download the Direct Deposit Form.
- Fill it out (don't worry about fitting one character into each box and the entity you are authorizing is Social Good Fund), sign it, and save it with your name at the beginning of the title, like so: "Jane Doe 2023 Direct Deposit Form."
- Email the signed W-9 and direct deposit forms to Techtonica (reply-all) in the application process thread (if you'll be receiving a stipend, otherwise, please just check off). This is due one week before your Onboarding Day (no later than Monday, Monday, December 29th at 5pm Pacific)
- If you live in California and would like an on-the-job training (OJT) certificate from the Office of Apprenticeship Standards, please fill out this form electronically (but not the evaluation part) and submit it to info@techtonica.org (otherwise, please just check off)

### If you've gotten a new laptop from the Part 1: Web Dev Foundations program, set up your new laptop.

- Install VS Code
- Add your pronouns to your Zoom profile
- Set Chrome as the default browser
- Install Git
- GitHub - make sure your Terminal is connected to your GitHub account so when you make a commit, GitHub knows it was from you.
- Install the LanguageTool Chrome
- Install the VS Code extensions:
- Install the Video Speed Controller Chrome extension and the Jiffy Reader extension
- For security reasons: adhere a movable camera cover (a Post-it note works for this)
- Install Rectangle for quickly resizing windows
- Set up a password so your laptop can't be used without it
- Install Homebrew
- Install npm
- Install nvm
- Install Node using `nvm install node`
- Download and install Postman
- Download and install Slack (then follow setup below)
- See if you can secure a monitor, mouse, keyboard, and any necessary cables on Buy Nothing, Freecycle, Craigslist, from a friend, etc.

## Onboarding Day (due Monday, January 5th 5:00 - 8:00pm)

Monday, January 5th at 5:00 p.m. Pacific Time. Onboarding day is the first day of the Part-Time Part 2: Software Applications Program.

### Get to know people

- Memorize your cohort's names and pronouns (you can use our pronoun practice sheet here).

### Connect with & rate mentors and practice your 30 second personal intros

- Make note of each mentor's name and a bit about them.
- Practice your personal pitch to use during interviews and at graduation, which we will publish on our Medium account, in this 2026-H1 Medium profiles doc. Use a script like this: "I'm __________[name] and my pronouns are __________[pronouns]. I'm a former__________[old jobs] and I love to ___________[hobbies]. I'm interested in tech because __________. It's been hard for me to get into tech because __________. I'm excited about joining Techtonica because _________. I believe I'll make a good software engineer because _______./I want to contribute _______ to the tech industry."
- Ask for any feedback to incorporate in your next round.
- After all the mentor rotations, fill out this form to rate the mentors in your group on commitment, empathy, flexibility, focus, growth mindset, listening, respect, and general fit.
- Meet with your assigned mentor during your pre-work
- Set up regular meeting times with mentors

### Using the Techtonica Slack

- Check out the `#2026-h1-participants` and `#2025-h2-2026-h1-tech-help` channels - your main channels.
- Review Techtonica's Slack tips.

## Post-Onboarding Day (due Monday, January 12th)

### Mentors

- Once assigned, meet with your assigned mentor during your first week to set up your weekly check-in times (before 5:00pm or after 9:00pm Pacific Times on weekdays and whatever times on weekends) and finalize your intro.
- Take a screenshot with your mentor and send it to info@techtonica.org titled "First Mentor-Mentee Meeting: <participant_name> & <mentor_name>", this is due on Monday, January 12th at 5pm Pacific Time.

### RSVP for Techtonica's events & meetings

- RSVP yes for the 2026-H1 Launch Celebration on Tuesday, January 27, 2026, at 5 p.m. to 6:00 p.m. Pacific Time.
- Add your cohort calendar to your Google Calendar (the link will be submitted in the first week of the program

## Onboarding Process for Part 1: Web Dev Foundations (2025-H2 cohort)

Congratulations on being accepted to Techtonica! Here are the things we need you to do before the program starts. Please check off each to-do when you've completed it or it doesn't apply to you. First day of Part 1: Web Dev Foundations program is Tuesday, October 14th at 5:00 p.m. Pacific Time.

## Onboarding Day (due Monday, September 15th 6:00 - 8:00pm)

Note: If you applied after September 10th, your onboarding day is September 22nd 6pm - 8pm.

### Get to know people

- Memorize your cohort's names and pronouns (you can use our pronoun practice sheet here).

### Practice your 30 second personal intros

- Prepare your one-minute personal intro/profile, which you will present at the launch celebration, during interviews, and at demo day. Use a script like this: "I'm __________[name] and my pronouns are __________[pronouns]. I'm a former__________[old jobs] and I love to ___________[hobbies]. I'm interested in tech because __________. It's been hard for me to get into tech because __________. I'm excited about joining Techtonica because _________. I believe I'll make a good software engineer because _______./I want to contribute _______ to the tech industry."
- Ask for any feedback to incorporate in your next round.

### Using the Techtonica Slack

- Add your pronouns to your profile and upload a clear photo of your face.
- Check out the `#2025-h2-participants` and `#2025-h2-2026-h1-tech-help` channels - your main channels.
- Download and install Slack and look over Techtonica's Slack tips.
- Ensure that your applicant name and pronouns are what others will see
- Add your phonetic name spelling and audible pronunciation to your Slack profile
- If you have more than one profile, ensure that you notify staff of which profile should be deactivated.

## After Onboarding Day (due October, 10th)

### Pre-Work starts

- Work through the pre-work and ask mentors and each other for any help.
- Track your progress in the 2025-H2- Pre-Work Checklist.
- Install Postgres and create your username database (we highly recommend that you ask one of the mentors for help for this process)
- White participants
- if you would like your own copy of the Me & White Supremacy workbook and need support to buy it, please reach out to Techtonica staff to order it for you
- Antiracism Resources for White Allies
- Start thinking about your Wellness Recovery Action Plan (WRAP template) you will be asked to complete this on day 1 of program

### Set up your laptop and platform accounts (All participants)

- Install VS Code
- Add your pronouns to your Zoom profile
- Set Chrome as the default browser
- Install Git
- GitHub - sign up, then create 3 repos. 1) A public techtonica-assignments repo: 2) A private techtonica-knowledge-check repo and 3) a public JS-exercises repo add the STEM to those repos, and make sure your Terminal is connected to your GitHub account so when you make a commit, GitHub knows it was from you.
- Sign up for Stack Overflow
- Install the LanguageTool Chrome
- Install the VS Code extensions:
- Install the Video Speed Controller Chrome extension and the Jiffy Reader extension
- Alternative text to speech extensions include Selection Reader, Reader View, Just Read, Natural Reader, and Speechify
- Set Gmail to default to reply-all and to display an undo button
- Add the Techtonica Events calendar to your Google Calendar
- For security reasons: adhere a movable camera cover (a Post-it note works for this)
- Install Rectangle for quickly resizing windows
- Set up a password so your laptop can't be used without it
- Install Homebrew
- Install npm
- Install nvm
- Install Node using `nvm install node`
- Download and install Postman
- Improve your typing speed, accuracy, and finger placement with The Typing Cat or Ratatype (you should be at 50+ WPM by the start of the program)
- See if you can secure a monitor, mouse, keyboard, and any necessary cables on Buy Nothing, Freecycle, Craigslist, from a friend, etc.
- Schedule times to get to know the STEM [please schedule these calls anytime between the time of your beginning onboarding tasks by Friday, October 10th at 8am PT]. Here are some good guiding questions:
- Life story? Greatest accomplishment? What is it important for people who are getting to know you to know about you?
- Fears for the program?
- Advice for the program?
- Deal with frustration?
- Most looking forward to?

### Update your social media accounts, LinkedIn, GitHub

- If you feel comfortable, create or update your social media accounts to say you're a Software Engineer in Training at @TechtonicaOrg.
- MANDATORY: Update your LinkedIn with info about Techtonica, like so:
- Add new experience with "Software Engineer in Training" as the title.
- Type "Techtonica" as the company and select Techtonica from the list of companies (it's the one with the blue image).
- Add this paragraph to the description:

  Techtonica is six months of full-time [five months for the 2024-H2 cohort], hands-on, project-based, collaborative software engineering learning with a focus on full-stack JavaScript. The last month is spent preparing to join Techtonica's partner companies for employment.

- Also, please beware that LinkedIn scammers will see whenever you add a new company and you may receive fraud text messages from team members. This has also happened with Techtonica, so please remember we use Slack and email, not texts. You should always confirm via Slack or email if you're unsure.
- If your LinkedIn URL is not easily remembered, edit it to be more simple.
- Share the full links (not handles) for your social media, LinkedIn, and GitHub in the 2025-H2- Pre-Work Checklist.

### Take and submit your picture

- Have your photos taken via screenshot or a camera phone using the photo guidelines outlined on the form below. That means you should wear clothes with solid colors like teal, cobalt blue, purple, or berry - please no patterns or logos.
- Have this white background ready on your zoom and plenty of natural light in front of you (but no sunlight directly on you).
- Remove anything you don't want in the pictures (like a coat or hat, anything with a pattern or logo, or things in the background).
- Be far enough back from the camera that Techtonica can crop your photo for consistency with others. Your not-tilted head should be in the center with space above it, your hands should be down by your sides or folded, and your shoulders should reach either side of the square photo.
- Check teeth, hair, lipstick, etc.
- Check off each of the following photos:
- One of your head slightly turned to the left (but still looking at the camera)
- One of your head slightly turned to the right
- One of your head facing the camera straight-on
- Please submit your photo using this form: (Please note that the deadline for turning in your photo is prior to day 1, Friday, October 10th at 8am PT).

### RSVP for Techtonica's events & meetings

- Accept invitations to study sessions you can make it to, or plan to check in on Slack if you can't make it. (Please sign up for the event in the same link as a volunteer but use the Techtonica applicant ticket).
- Add your cohort calendar to your Google Calendar
